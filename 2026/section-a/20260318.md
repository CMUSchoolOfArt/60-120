### Advanced Bitsy Tricks and Hacks

Bitsy is more versatile than you might think, and is also highly extensible. If you do some digging you can learn how to use built-in features to [have conversations](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ajZRsuhx9rU&list=PLlXuD3kyVEr6bprFqUBCOEWT47xqYmVQ4&index=11&pp=iAQB0gcJCcUKAYcqIYzv), maintain an [inventory of items](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cjfPvPzUJ0o&list=PLlXuD3kyVEr6bprFqUBCOEWT47xqYmVQ4&index=12&t=2s&pp=iAQB), say and [respond to item counts](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=14yhWrpvWxA&list=PLlXuD3kyVEr6bprFqUBCOEWT47xqYmVQ4&index=24&pp=iAQB), use [variables](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C40pr2UAVrc&list=PLlXuD3kyVEr6bprFqUBCOEWT47xqYmVQ4&index=26&pp=iAQB), have your [avatar change over time](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=21NvGyoGtYg&list=PLlXuD3kyVEr6bprFqUBCOEWT47xqYmVQ4&index=28&pp=iAQB), and much more. There are also [dozens of publicly-contributed "hacks"](https://github.com/seleb/bitsy-hacks) that allow you to change how the game works, but your mileage may vary in using them; save your work often.

1. [Conditionals (a keyed door)](https://github.com/CMUSchoolOfArt/60-120/tree/main/lectures/interactive_narrative/bitsy_hacks/conditionals)
2. [Extra Colors and Animation Frames](https://github.com/CMUSchoolOfArt/60-120/tree/main/lectures/interactive_narrative/bitsy_hacks/extra_colors_and_frames)
3. [Convert Images to Bitsy Rooms](https://github.com/CMUSchoolOfArt/60-120/tree/main/lectures/interactive_narrative/bitsy_hacks/image_to_bitsy)
4. [**HACK**: Directional Avatar](https://github.com/CMUSchoolOfArt/60-120/tree/main/lectures/interactive_narrative/bitsy_hacks/directional_avatar)
5. [**HACK**: Per-Room Audio in Bitsy](https://github.com/CMUSchoolOfArt/60-120/tree/main/lectures/interactive_narrative/bitsy_hacks/bitsy_audio)

### Upcoming Events of Possible Interest

#### Uncanny Tools Workshops, 3/28-29

During the weekend of March 27-29, the CFA is hosting [**Uncanny Tools**](https://www.architecture.cmu.edu/events/uncanny-tools-workshop-series): a workshop series that explores AI as a strange new instrument for alternative practices. Free lunch is included. Registration is now open; apply before Wednesday, March 25 using [**THIS FORM**](https://forms.gle/QsHCpdJa9kWyXUdJ9). The workshops include:

* **Dadaist Filmmaking: Re-Editing the Readymade Film** — [Derrick Schultz](https://artificial-images.com/). Build a shared video corpus, tag it with AI tools, and resequence clips using absurdist rules that ignore traditional continuity.
* **Blooming from Noise: Digging into the Roots of AI-Generated Images** — Oscar Keyes (VCU). Co-construct a small-scale, low-energy image model from locally sourced photographs, and dig into the mechanics behind diffusion models.
* **Slopera: AI Slop Operas** — Brian Bird & Ethan Nevidomsky (DeepLocal). Design AI characters loaded with contradictions, throw them into absurd scenarios, and render the chaos as short audio dramas on Raspberry Pis.
* **Image Sabotage: Unfolding the Mediated Reality** — Jimmy Cheng (CMU.) Construct your own viewpoint from a shared dataset and surface alternative narratives hidden in how AI organizes reality.

#### SOMA Immersive Storytelling Festival

From **April 2-4**, the Dietrich College is presenting the second edition of the [**SONA Immersive Storytelling Festival**](https://www.sonafestival.com/); [registration is now open](https://www.sonafestival.com/registration).

> SONA showcases groundbreaking VR, AR, and 360° storytelling, bringing together creators, technologists, and audiences to explore the future of immersive media. There will be talks, panels,  a VR showcase, and more.
