# Chapbook Rubric

* Rubric: AI-Assisted Artist Book / Monograph
* This rubric is weighted toward thinking, authorship, and execution, not vibes.
* Total: 100 points

---

## 1. Concept, Intent, and Authorship (25 pts)

*Whether the student is actually making a book about something, for someone, with a point of view.*

### Excellent (22–25)

* Clear, specific premise or motivation.
* Distinct narrative or conceptual throughline across pages.
* Book reads as a deliberate object, not a pile of images.
* Evidence of authorial choice: inclusion, exclusion, pacing.

### Adequate (14–21)

* General idea present, but vague or underdeveloped.
* Some narrative coherence, but inconsistent or thin.
* Relies heavily on surface appeal.

### Poor (0–13)

* No discernible concept or audience.
* Book feels autogenerated or arbitrary.
* Confuses “looks professional” with “has meaning.”

---

## 2. Use of AI: Critical or Inventive Engagement (20 pts)

*Whether AI is treated as a medium with constraints, bias, and affordances rather than a magic aesthetic button.*

#### Excellent (17–20)

* AI use is intentional, exploratory, or critical.
* Prompts, iteration, selection, and editing show judgment.
* Images are shaped toward an idea, not accepted as-is.
* Avoids default commercial aesthetics unless explicitly critiqued.

#### Adequate (10–16)

* Competent image generation but limited experimentation.
* Some reliance on stock AI styles.
* Minimal critical reflection.

#### Poor (0–9)

* Slop.
* Glossy-advertising look mistaken for artistry.
* No evidence of iteration or discernment.
* Uncritical reproduction of harmful tropes or aesthetics.

---

## 3. Book Thinking and Artist Book Awareness (15 pts)

*Whether the student understands the book as a form.*

#### Excellent (13–15)

* Thoughtful sequencing, rhythm, and pacing.
* Pages relate to each other spatially and narratively.
* Uses repetition, variation, or structure intentionally.
* Shows awareness that books are time-based objects.

#### Adequate (7–12)

* Some attention to sequence, but inconsistent.
* Pages often interchangeable.
* Book reads like a slideshow.

#### Poor (0–6)

* No sequencing logic.
* Pages operate independently.
* Weak evidence of understanding books as a medium.


---

## 4. Layout, Typography, and InDesign Execution (20 pts)

*Practical design competence and attention to detail.*

#### Excellent (17–20)

* Full-bleed handled correctly, if appropriate
* Consistent margins, alignment, and typography.
* No accidental crops, gutters, or scaling errors.
* Layout decisions support content.

#### Adequate (10–16)

* Layout mostly functional but sloppy in places.
* Inconsistent spacing or typographic choices.
* Errors suggest rushed execution.

#### Poor (0–9)

* Technical mistakes throughout.
* Failure to follow template requirements.
* Neglect of basic layout discipline.

---

## 5. Print-on-Demand Execution (10 pts)

*Ability to operate within real production constraints.*

#### Excellent (9–10)

* Lulu page set up correctly.
* Appropriate metadata, keywords, and visibility.
* Files prepared to spec.

#### Adequate (5–8)

* Functional but careless setup.
* Metadata incomplete or generic.

#### Poor (0–4)

* Broken links, incorrect settings, missing requirements.
* Demonstrates lack of attention to instructions.

---

## 6. Reflection and Self-Assessment (10 pts)

*Ability to evaluate one’s own work honestly.*

#### Excellent (9–10)

* Specific, grounded reflection.
* Identifies real successes and failures.
* Shows learning and awareness.

#### Adequate (5–8)

* General reflections.
* Limited specificity.

#### Poor (0–4)

* Vague praise of own work.
* No critical distance.
